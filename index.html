Dr. John Houbolt

The man who's lunar-orbit rendezvous "saved the government $20 billion."

Among his many achievements, Dr. Houbolt was an avid mathematician, and in researching and teaching himself orbital mechanics, he realized that the concept of Lunar Orbit Rendezvous (LOR) had far-reaching ramifications in space travel. He deemed this idea so important that he risked his career promoting the concept of LOR.

Here's a time line of Dr. Houbolt's life:

  • 1919 - Born in Altoona, Iowa
  • 1942 - Earned MS degree in Civil Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign
  • 1943 - Began his career as an engineer in the Structures Research Division at the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA)
  • 1949 - Associate Chief of the Dynamic Loads Division
  • 1957 - Earned Ph.D at Swiss Federal Institute of Technology, Zurich (ETH)
  • 1961 - Chief of the Theoretical Mechanics Division
  • 1963 - Received the NASA Exceptional Scientific Achievement Award for the conception and development of the LOR. Left NASA for position of Senior Vice President and Senior Consultant at Aeronautical Research Associates of Princeton
  • 1976 - Returned as Chief Aeronautical Scientist at NASA
  • 1984 - Retired from NASA
  • 2014 - Died in Scarborough, ME after celebrating his 95th birthday

"I can safely say I'm shaking hands with the man who single-handedly saved the government $20 billion."

-- Ed Garrock - NASA
